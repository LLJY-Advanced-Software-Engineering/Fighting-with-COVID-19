PPE - Forecasting

The University of Florida Health created their own PPE calculator.

Allowing them to calculate current demand and forecast future worst case scenario needs.

PPE Forecasting

This model can be applied to any PPE with multiple data sources.
 
According to the labor demand of each hospital and the assumption of personal protective equipment for each role in each shift, the patient input ratio is modified.
 
The daily personal protective equipment demand is automatically calculated according to the patient classification.
 
The Information was obtained from The American Hospital Association (AHA) and AHRMM, and was not developed by LLJY.

About the PPE

Personal Protective Equipment

It's one crucial way to both stop the spread of COVID-19 and keep the healthcare workers (doctors, nurses, other caregivers) currently on the pandemic's front-lines safe.
PPE works as a barrier between an individual’s skin, mouth, nose, or eyes and viral and bacterial infections.

Estiamted PPE

The estimated numbers of PPE are based on COVID-19 cases on health regions in Canada, for reference only. Our team will not be responsible for any negative consequences or extra costs. Any use of the Information is at the user’s own risk.
